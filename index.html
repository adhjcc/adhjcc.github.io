SFM-TTS: Lightweight and Rapid Speech Synthesis with Flexible Shortcut Flow Matching

In recent years, diffusion models such as denoising diffusion probabilistic models and flow matching have made significant progress in speech synthesis. However, these models exhibit a remarkable decline in generation quality when the number of steps is relatively small. Consequently, producing high-quality speech requires a considerably longer generation time and higher computing costs. In this paper, we introduce SFM-TTS, an ODE-based Text-to-Speech model leveraging shortcut model. This approach transforms standard Gaussian distributions into ground truth distributions via linear interpolations, allowing the model to skip ahead in the generation process and achieving high-quality speech synthesis in the few- and one-step generations. Additionally, the fast linear attention with a single head is adopted to minimize the number of parameters instead of standard Transformer, which resulting more lightweight model size. Experimental results demonstrate that our proposed model attains the smallest model size, while still preserving the fidelity and naturalness of the generated speech with fewer generation steps.
